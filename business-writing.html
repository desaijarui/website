Business Writing

Entity Relationship Diagrams (ERDs)

ERD diagrams are critical to make because they show the relationship between different entity sets stored in a database. These diagrams usually illustrate the logical structure of the databases, which is why they are important for a business to make. 

Class Diagrams

Class diagrams by themselves should be made before the entity relationship diagrams are created. This is because all class diagrams then come together to create create an entity relationship diagram. They show the relationships and power of various objects within a project.

The first class diagram goes for the Polls. This includes both the user’s ability to participate in polling and then the KHBPA’s ability to post the poll results on the website for public access. This will allow others to see the results of the poll and use that information if they need to.

The second class diagram goes for the Photo Comments. This feature goes to show the processes that are involved in adding comments to the photographs that are available on the website for public use. This can help to enhance the marketing for this website if used correctly. 

The third class diagram goes to provide Online Forms. This includes allowing users to both download and fill-out forms online, and submit the document once it has been filled out and scanned into the computer.  This will allow for larger processes to be run.

The fourth class diagram goes to provide Event Feedback. This will allow users to provide feedback directly to the KHBPA and will show the processes which are involved with that step.

Use Case Diagrams

Use case diagrams are important to create for a business because these diagrams overview the usage requirements of a system. They are helpful when presented to management or project stakeholders. These diagrams depict the use case, the actors, and the associations between them all. 

When user accounts are created, often comes a need to update the information associated with the account. To do that, there needs to be a capability of the website to allow a user to update account information.

To prevent unwanted changes or destruction of data, users must have the capability to logout of their accounts. This use case diagram outlines the steps to sucessfully logout of a user account while still remaining on the website.

Sequence Diagrams

Sequence diagrams are critical for an organization because these diagrams show how objects operate with one another and in what order. These charts show the exact sequence that an interaction will follow in order to complete the task. Being able to use these diagrams help to visualize the steps easier.

Process Models

Process models are important to an organization because they can be used in various contexts. These models represent a process of an enterprise, so they can either be a current process or a future process. These help to plan out what needs to be accomplished in the future in regards to the overall business process.

The as-is process chart shows the current functionality of the KHBPA’s website. Most business processes lead to a web page that is entirely text based. This business process model is what we will use as a guide to improve upon the business processes in the new system.

The to-be process chart depicts how we think the new site should work and is modeled on the current model. The main driver of maintaining a similar layout is because we do not want to make the experience more difficult for users, as they are already familiar with using the old website. This embodies the projected features that fulfill many of our system requirements and provides value as outlined in the system request.

Trace Matrix

Having a trace matrix in a project is important because it is a project management tool that aligns use cases with the system requirements. The purpose of this tool is to ensure that no requirements are lost during development and validation.

Risk Analysis

A risk analysis is a process of assessing the likelihood that an adverse event is going to occur with a process within the business. These are important to conduct because you can assess whether it is worth or not worth the risk to take a chance on a new development or process. 

        The following is a risk analysis for the Kentucky Horsemen’s Benevolent and Protective Association’s website development project. These risks are dependencies that could hinder the development and implementation of the system or prevent it altogether. There is criteria to assess the risks, determining whether they are high or low risk areas, and each risk has been categorized as based on this criteria with a focus on high risk items.

Use Case Analysis:
    1. Donate Online – Donating members (High Risk)
    2. Access mobile optimized website – Smartphone users (High Risk)
    3. Retrieve revenue reports from payment processor – Staff (High Risk)
    4. Manage Photo gallery – KHBPA Staff(High Risk)
    5. Register members online – Potential members (High Risk)
     6. Backup – Staff (store data for business continuity) (High Risk)  
    7. Recovery – Staff (retrieve data for business continuity) (High Risk)
    8. Create Profiles online – Trainers, owners, horsemen (to create, view, and manage profiles)              (High Risk)
    9. Login – Staff (web content management tool and database) (High Risk)
    10. Process Claims – Members (High Risk) 2. Add benefits to members - KHBPA Staff (High                 Risk)
    11. Update news articles – Website visitors (High Risk)
    12. Add benefits to members - KHBPA Staff (High Risk)
    13. Link website to social media – Website visitors (Low Risk)
    14. Access important forms online – Members (High Risk)
    15. Create calendar of events – Website visitors (Low Risk)
    16. Pay Invoices online – Members (High Risk)
    17. Pay Fees online (High Risk)
    18. Poll Users – Members (Low Risk)
    19. Create better Business continuity – KHBPA staff (High Risk)     20. Create Database - KHBPA Staff (to organize members/staff/trainer records) (High Risk)  
    21. Link website to other important websites – Website Visitors (Low Risk)
    22. Allow User feedback – Members (Low Risk)
    23. Improve color scheme of website – KHBPA (Low Risk)

Other Risks:
    1. Disaster Recovery (Vision) - High Risk
            a. This is a high-risk since it affects all business processes in the event of bad system update, system failure and/or accidental deletion by staff error. It also is a requirement of system/business continuity and integrity.
    2. Drive Traffic (Visions) - High Risk
            a. This is a high-risk item because the client needed this functionality for the system to be able to function properly for their clients can go to different website in order to give appropriate information and stream line data retrieval for their clients.
    3. Contract with GoDaddy for Domain (Arch. Consider.) - High Risk
            a. This is a high-risk item because the domain is required for outside (including the facility in this case since not on internal network) for connectivity of website and the system.
    4. Securing a contract with Microsoft Azure for a hosting machine (Arch. Consider.) - High Risk
            a. This is a high-risk item because this system will hold the database along with backing up the database.
    5. Acquire WordPress on a server (Arch. Consider.) - High Risk
            a. This is a requirement for the Content Management System to have an easy to use and maintain website.
    6. Get contracts with ClearDB and PayPal (Arch. Consider.) - High Risks
            a. These are requirements for system functionality to interface with Microsoft Azure and receive payments/donations.

How Risks will be Addressed
 
 Risk will be addressed through elaboration phase through thorough planning with use case, sequence, and class diagrams. These will help us visualize the business processes and locate critical points of the business processes. Having these in place will not only help us complete each task faster, but everyone (now and in the future) will understand the pieces of the system and the processes within. This will address risk because developers will be able to follow the diagrams to find the high risk items.
